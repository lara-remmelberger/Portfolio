Interactive Media & Game Development

Breathing Underwater – An Animated Story Game

Unity-based Narrative Experience | Animated Story Game

"Breathing Underwater" is an animated story game that explores alienation through a surrealist lens. Players navigate a protagonist’s mundane routines as his reality gradually warps into the uncanny -from telephones dangling from ceilings to strangers with goldfish bowls for heads.

Built in Unity, the project focuses on narrative branching and player agency. Through critical dialogue choices, players directly shape the emotional arc, experiencing the psychological tension of estrangement firsthand.

Narrative Design:
Implementation of branching storylines and multiple endings
Technical Art:
Blending hand-drawn 2D aesthetics with interactive logic in Unity

Interaction Design & Hardware Prototyping: Media Bias - The Weight of Your Trust

Concept & Vision

Duo-Project | Seminar Brief: Interactive AI-reflection in museums.

"Media Bias" explores the "Weight of Trust" - investigating how an information's medium dictates our perception of its trustworthiness. While AI faces modern skepticism, this installation reminds visitors that books, radio, and the internet once faced identical doubt. By comparing a historical media timeline with a real-time binary vote -"AI vs. Media of the Past"- the exhibit prompts visitors to deconstruct their own preconceptions and reflect on why they trust certain media more than others. A physical voting system translates abstract opinions into mechanical movement: an antique-style scale tilts in real-time, giving a tangible form to the digital debate.

1 / 4

Technical Implementation

Hardware Stack: Powered by a Raspberry Pi Pico W (MicroPython), using a stepper motor for precise scale tilting and an OLED display for real-time vote tracking.

Interaction Design: Visitors cast their vote via two large-scale buttons, deciding whether to trust AI as an information source. The current vote ratio is instantly visualized by the tilting scale and digital display.

Context: A curated timeline of historical quotes (Plato to Pulitzer) frames modern AI skepticism as a recurring media pattern.

Project Type: Collaborative Duo-Project with N. Menzl

Key Contributions: Concept, Hardware Programming, Interaction Design

Short Film Production

PULSE

Collaborative Short Film

Showcased at the 4th International Technical University of Kenya - Exploring Visual Cultures Conference of Artificial Intelligence, "PULSE" is a sci-fi short film exploring humanity's over-reliance on technology. Set in an exaggerated future, society has surrendered all critical thinking to AI assistants.
When the central system temporarily fails, absurd chaos ensues: citizens are left paralyzed without algorithmic guidance, resulting in people commuting in their pajamas or waiting for non-existent traffic. The narrative follows a protagonist who attempts to break free from this failing system, only to discover the grim reality of his lost autonomy.

Concept & Script, Production, Creative Direction, 2nd Camera

Post-Production: Editing & Audio Design (Scene 06:38 – 09:10)

NEXUS

Collaborative Short Film | Concept & Production

Awarded "Best Movie" and "Best Screenplay" at the LMU Oscars, "NEXUS" is a genre-bending short film exploring the theme of "the rift". The narrative follows a protagonist who escapes his reality by slipping into various cinematic universes, always confronting the same villain.
In reality, the protagonist is simply a man facing bankruptcy. To cope with his situation, he retreats into movies and reimagines the local mail carrier, who delivers his bills and eviction notices, as his ultimate nemesis. The project serves as a satirical take on escapism and classic movie tropes.

Awards: LMU Oscar for Best Movie & Best Screenplay
Co-Roles: Concept & Script, Production, Creative Direction, 2nd Camera, Post-Production
